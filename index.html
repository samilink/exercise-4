Six types of Shoes

Ballet Shoes  |   Sneakers   |   Heels   |   Toe Shoes   |   Ski Boot

Ballet Shoes

A ballet shoe, or ballet slipper, is a lightweight shoe designed specifically for ballet dancing. It may be made from soft leather, canvas, or satin, and has flexible, thin full or split soles.

Crocs

Crocs, Inc. is an American footwear company based in Broomfield, Colorado, that manufactures and markets the Crocs brand of foam footwear. Crocs Inc term these "clogs", but they do not contain any wood like traditional clogs.

Sneakers

Sneakers (US) or trainers (UK), also known by a wide variety of other names, are shoes primarily designed for sports or other forms of physical exercise but which are also widely used for everyday casual wear.

Heels

High-heeled shoes, also known as high heels, are a type of shoe with an upward-angled sole. The heel in such shoes is raised above the ball of the foot.

Toe Shoe

Minimalist shoes are shoes intended to closely approximate barefoot running or walking conditions in comparison to traditional shoes.

Ski Boot

Ski boots are footwear used in skiing to provide a way to attach the skier to skis using ski bindings. The ski/boot/binding combination is used to effectively transmit control inputs from the skier's legs to the snow.
